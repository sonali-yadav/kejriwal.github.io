About The Man Himself

Born on August 16, 1968, in an educated family in Siwani, Bhivani District, Haryana- India, Arvind Kejriwal is an Indian Politician and a former bureaucrat who is the current Chief Minister of the state of Delhi.

He is also the national convener of the Aam Aadmi Party which won the 2015 Delhi elections with 67 out of 70 seats.

He is a founding member of a movement named Parivartan (meaning "Change") in Sundar Nagar, Delhi which addresses citizens' grievances related to public works, electricity, income tax, social welfare, etc.

He has become a very popular Indian Leader now because of the kind of reforms that he has brought about in Delhi.

Some of his ideas like the Odd-Even formula, Mohalla Clinics, Govenment School Reforms, etc have really left a lasting impact on people's minds and have established him as the "People's Man".

Education & Career

  • He was educated at the Campus School in Hisar and at Christian Missionary Holy Child School at Sonipat in Haryana.
  • Cracked the IIT-JEE exam in 1985 and scored an all India rand of 563. He then graduated from IIT Kharagpur as a Mechanical Engineer.
  • Joined Tata Steel in 1989 and was posted in Jamshedpur. He resigned in 1992 to prepare for the Civil Services Examination.
  • Joined the IRS as an Assistant Commisioner of Income Tax in 1995 through the Civil Services Examinations.
  • In Feb, 2006, he resigned from his post of Joint Commissioner of Income Tax in New Delhi.

Anti Corruption Activities

  • Co-Founded "Parivartan" in 1999 along with Manish Sisodia.
  • Along with other social activists like Anna Hazare, Aruna Roy and Shekhar Singh, Kejriwal came to be recognised as an important contributor to the campaign for a national-level Right to Information Act (enacted in 2005)
  • In December 2006, Kejriwal established the Public Cause Research Foundation in December 2006, together with Manish Sisodia and Abhinandan Sekhri.
  • In 2011, Kejriwal joined several other activists, including Anna Hazare and Kiran Bedi, to form the India Against Corruption (IAC) group. The IAC demanded enactment of the Jan Lokpal Bill. The campaign evolved into the 2011 Indian anti-corruption movement.

Political Career

  • In November 2012, the Aam Aadmi Party was formally launched and Kejriwal was elected as the party's National Convener.
  • Kejriwal became the fifth most-mentioned Indian politician on social media channels in the run-up to the Delhi Legislative Assembly elections, 2013, with Kejriwal contesting against the incumbent Chief Minister Sheila Dikshit.
  • Kejriwal was sworn in as the second-youngest chief minister of Delhi on 28 December 2013, as AAP formed a coalition with BJP.
  • On 14 February 2014 he resigned as Chief Minister after failing to table the Jan Lokpal Bill in the Delhi Assembly.
  • Kejriwal led Aam Aadmi Party to win 67 of the 70 constituencies in the 2015 Delhi Assembly elections.
  • He took oath on 14 February 2015 as Delhi's chief minister for a second time at Ramlila Maidan. Since then his party has passed the Jan Lokpal Bill though with some differences.
